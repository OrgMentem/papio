Abstract

Purpose

This study aims to investigate the evolving role and impact of ChatGPT in higher education. It seeks to understand the applications, benefits and challenges associated with ChatGPT, focusing on its potential to enhance teaching, learning and assessment while addressing ethical considerations in the educational context.

TOKEN

Using a comprehensive literature review approach, this study systematically examined 29 existing studies and scholarly works related to ChatGPT in higher education. Synthesizing these findings offers a multifaceted view of the subject, encompassing applications, advantages, limitations and ethical implications.

Findings

The study indicates that ChatGPT can have a significant impact on personalized learning, lesson planning, providing feedback, creating study materials, generating quizzes and exam questions, supporting language learning, offering virtual teaching assistance, tutoring and fostering critical thinking. However, it also sheds light on the ethical issues and challenges associated with its use, including privacy and data protection, transparency and alignment with educational principles.

Practical implications

This study underscores the practical applications of ChatGPT in higher education, offering insights that can significantly enhance teaching, learning and assessment practices. By leveraging ChatGPT, institutions can personalize learning experiences and provide tailored feedback and streamlined assessment processes, thereby improving student engagement and understanding. In addition, the integration of ChatGPT as a virtual teaching assistant can enhance teaching effectiveness and efficiency by supporting classroom activities, providing additional resources and answering students’ questions in real-time.

Originality/value

The originality of this study lies in its comprehensive exploration of ChatGPT’s applications in higher education, offering a balanced perspective on opportunities and ethical considerations. By providing valuable insights, it equips educators and institutions with a deeper understanding of the ChatGPT’s potential and challenges in the educational landscape.

1. Introduction

In recent years, there has been significant progress in the field of artificial intelligence (AI), leading to its widespread use in various industries such as the healthcare and education sectors. AI systems are equipped with the ability to replicate human cognitive processes and execute intricate tasks through the manipulation of large volumes of information (Benuyenah, 2023; McFarlane and Latorella, 2002). For example, in healthcare, AI can be used to support the work of medical professionals by examining patient records, interpreting medical images and identifying potential health concerns (Bohr and Memarzadeh, 2020). This technology can provide valuable insights and support to healthcare providers, enabling them to make more informed decisions and improve patient care. Similarly, in education, AI is used to enhance academic and administrative services, including intelligent tutoring systems (ITS) that provide personalized one-on-one tutoring experiences (Chen et al., 2020). A comprehensive analysis has shown that these ITS generally have a positive and moderate impact on the academic performance of college students. However, the formulation of an efficient ITS involves a multifaceted process that surpasses the scope of just content and design, encompassing the fine-tuning of feedback delivery and dialogue tactics (Ilieva et al., 2023).

ChatGPT, a recent invention from OpenAI as a conversational chatbot, has the potential to streamline the use of AI in education. Owing to its exceptional natural language processing capacity, ChatGPT can offer replies that are deemed human-like and renowned globally for their coherent, structured and informative character (Javaid et al., 2023a). As a notable achievement, ChatGPT managed to pass four exams at the University of Minnesota Law School, even though the scores were not very high. These outcomes suggest that ChatGPT could potentially earn a university degree (Bhayana et al., 2023). Since its launch on November 30, 2022, ChatGPT has rapidly become the fastest-growing user application in history, reaching 100 million active users within just two months of its release, as reported in January 2023 (Chen, 2023).

Although ChatGPT has been successful in its applications, it has also introduced new challenges and threats to education. The utilization of its capability to offer precise answers to user inquiries raises concerns about AI-aided cheating, as it may be used to complete written assignments, projects, homeworks and examinations on behalf of students (Morris, 2023). It has been reported that certain educational institutions have prohibited access to ChatGPT on their premises (Bin-Nashwan et al., 2023). A study conducted by Mhlanga (2023), examined the potential consequences of ChatGPT in the field of education, uncovering apprehensions among educators regarding its utilization (Wardat et al., 2023). Educational institutions were worried that students might turn to ChatGPT for quick content generation, potentially outsourcing their assignments and projects. It is imperative to use ChatGPT responsibly and ethically, as highlighted by (Lund and Wang, 2023). A critical review of 60 articles on ChatGPT in healthcare, medical education and academia by Sallam (2023) uncovered various issues, such as plagiarism and inaccurate citations, which necessitate immediate attention to maximize the benefits and minimize the risks of ChatGPT-assisted learning (Lo, 2023).

Therefore, the main goal of this research was to delve deeper into the impact of incorporating ChatGPT into higher education. In addition, we aimed to shed light on the challenges and limitations of effectively using ChatGPT as an educational tool in teaching and assessment. The study also aimed to highlight the ethical implications of integrating AI and ChatGPT in educational settings. Through this research endeavor, our intention was to fill the existing gaps in academic knowledge and contribute to a more comprehensive understanding of how AI plays a role in education.

Moreover, gaining insights into how high school instructors and educators can make use of ChatGPT in their teaching and assessment process is of paramount importance since they are among the primary users and beneficiaries of this technology. The knowledge acquired in this study provides valuable information for educators, researchers and policymakers. It will help them grasp the implications of integrating ChatGPT into educational settings and promote responsible and efficient use of this transformative technology. As a result, this study is designed to address the following research questions:

RQ1.

How can educators use ChatGPT to improve teaching and assessment processes?

RQ2.

What are the potential limitations and challenges associated with integrating ChatGPT in education?

RQ3.

What are the ethical implications of AI and ChatGPT in higher education?

This study examined the application of ChatGPT in academic settings for teaching, learning and student evaluation. It begins with a summary of the ChatGPT concepts, followed by an explanation of the study’s methodology in Section 2, including data collection and analysis procedures. Section 3 presents the results and discusses their implications, and the final section offers recommendations and conclusions based on the literature.

2. Materials and methods

This study conducted a systematic review to explore the existing body of academic and professional literature concerning the application of AI in the realms of teaching and student assessment. Particular focus was placed on the role of ChatGPT in these contexts. The review process encompasses activities such as literature search, screening, selection, subsequent analysis and synthesis (Fink, 2019).

2.2 Data sources and literature search

In the initial phase, a thorough and comprehensive search was executed across a range of academic databases, including Google Scholar, Web of Science, Scopus, ScienceDirect and Multidisciplinary Digital Publishing Institute. The search aimed to identify pertinent articles, reports, conference proceedings and professional publications, as suggested by Meho and Yang (2007). Using a strategic approach, the search used various combinations of keywords and phrases, which encompassed terms like [(“ChatGPT OR Chatbot OR Chat Generative Pre-Trained Transformer”) AND (“Teaching OR Instruction OR Learning OR Assessment”) AND (“Challenges” OR “obstacles” OR “issues” OR “difficulties” OR Implications) AND (“Education” OR “academia” OR “high education”)]. Initially, the search yielded 892 articles; however, after eliminating 318 duplicates, 574 unique articles remained. These articles were thoroughly examined using predetermined inclusion and exclusion criteria based on the guidelines proposed by Meline (2006). Finally, 29 studies met these criteria and were included in the final analysis. The selection of these 29 articles was guided by stringent criteria to prioritize high-quality research that was directly relevant to our research questions and objectives. Our systematic literature review emphasized depth over breadth, focusing on a thorough analysis of a carefully chosen set of pertinent studies rather than an extensive array of potentially less relevant literature, as noted in prior studies (Aljarrah et al., 2021; AlShamsi et al., 2022; Karagozlu et al., 2023; Mohammad and Vargas, 2022; Wang et al., 2019). Consequently, the inclusion of 29 articles in the analysis was deemed to be appropriate and sufficient for the scope of this study. To guarantee the quality of research, it is essential to establish clear inclusion and exclusion criteria as well as to rigorously evaluate the research rigorously (Okoli and Schabram, 2015). 2.4 Analysis

We conducted a thorough analysis of the chosen articles, diligently extracting critical information and insights relevant to the application of ChatGPT in education for both teaching and evaluating students.

These works addressed the balance between using AI tools and maintaining academic integrity. In 2024, studies like Parker et al. Research now examines how to optimize AI’s role in evaluation processes while ensuring fairness and accuracy. 3.2 The use of AI and ChatGPT in higher education

There have emerged numerous potential applications of ChatGPT in educational settings, as evidenced by a study by (Zhai, 2022) which outlines three primary ways in which ChatGPT can be effectively used in education: personalizing learning experiences, automating administrative tasks and aiding in the teaching process. According to a representative from ChatGPT, the technology is already being used in classrooms to develop lessons, create quiz questions and provide feedback on students’ writing (Atlas, 2023). Furthermore, the concept of human-centered approach (humanics), advocates that technology can help students nurture their innate abilities to prepare for a future job market where they will collaborate with capable technologies. This approach encourages students to focus less on the fear of being replaced by technology and instead on learning how to use it effectively (Dwivedi et al., 2023).

The utilization of ChatGPT in education is manifest in multiple domains, offering advantages to both students and educators. Terwiesch (2023) carried out an evaluation of ChatGPT’s performance in MBA assessments, which demonstrated its outstanding ability in student exams, achieving an average grade of B for all questions. This study underscores the potential of the technology in generating exam questions expeditiously, although some level of human intervention may be required to enhance their quality. Given the ongoing discourse regarding skills in education, it is imperative to recognize that such technologies also have significant applications in the workplace. This underscores the importance of equipping students with the necessary skills to use these technologies effectively in making business decisions (Grassini, 2023).

In a similar study using ChatGPT to answer microeconomics exam questions, it demonstrated passable performance, correctly answering 19/30 in microeconomics and 26/30 in macroeconomics. This impressive accomplishment has placed it in the top echelon, surpassing the performance of 91% and 99% of the 3,255 and 2,789 students, respectively, who completed a full semester of microeconomics and macroeconomics (Mao et al., 2023). Another experiment in a law school setting found that ChatGPT could craft well-written essays, even in exam conditions, with a solid understanding of legal rules. Nonetheless, it fell short in terms of identifying pertinent issues and providing sufficient depth (Hargreaves, 2023). The study further exposed inconsistencies, highlighting the technology’s tendency to produce irrelevant or insufficiently detailed responses.

The utilization of ChatGPT in education may significantly alter conventional educational tasks and evaluations. Over the years, advancements in AI, particularly in machine learning and natural language processing, have been harnessed for personalized instruction (Parker et al., 2024). Studies on ChatGPT’s classroom applications elicited numerous possibilities for education and stimulated critical inquiries about the evolving roles of educators (Baidoo-Anu and Ansah, 2023; Gill et al., 2024; Grassini, 2023). Consequently, preparations are already underway to integrate ChatGPT into educational settings, such as the ChatGPT-assisted education platform recently announced by the Chinese company Meten Holding Group, which is expected to drive innovation in the education sector (Elbanna and Armstrong, 2023).

Therefore, it is important to note that educators should not view the integration of ChatGPT as a hindrance to their productivity in teaching. In fact, this technology is expected to have a significant impact on the business world. Therefore, it is crucial for educational institutions to adapt by placing more emphasis on managerial decision-making. This involves the process of developing and evaluating alternative solutions (Elbanna and Armstrong, 2023).

3.3 Harnessing ChatGPT for teaching in higher education

ChatGPT is a highly versatile tool in the realm of higher education teaching and has the potential to significantly benefit both educators and students. Its capabilities range from personalized learning experiences to streamlined lesson planning, comprehensive feedback on assignments and even virtual language instruction. In addition, ChatGPT can aid in research tasks, function as a virtual teaching assistant and provide critical thinking opportunities through engaging discussions and improve problem-solving skills (Rahman and Watanobe, 2023). These features not only improve the quality of education, but also make it more adaptable, engaging and effective for all parties involved. 3.3.1 Personalized learning.

ChatGPT is leading the way in a significant transformation in education, prioritizing the personalization of learning experiences. It is widely acknowledged that each student has a unique learning style, pace and set of strengths and weaknesses, and that ChatGPT is equipped to respond to these differences by adapting and tailoring content to cater to individual needs. According to the findings of (Hariri, 2023; Khan et al., 2023), AI-enhanced chatbots are instrumental in personalized learning, as they provide immediate, tailored support to students. By using chatbots to obtain customized practice questions, students can focus on strengthening their weaker areas. This innovative approach to learning is akin to having a virtual tutor who understands the specific learning journey of each student. Whether a student requires additional explanations for complex topics, more challenging assignments to stay engaged or specific feedback on their work, ChatGPT can provide exactly what is needed. According to (Javaid et al., 2023b), instructors may also use ChatGPT to formulate questions for classroom discussions pertaining to books, topics, historical events or ideas. (Adiguzel et al., 2023) noted that it enables educators to quickly generate engaging inquiries that cater to students’ diverse learning pace. In addition, ChatGPT may also provide various examples and practice opportunities to help students comprehend the subject or ideas more effectively (Rasul et al., 2023). This adaptability is a significant advantage as it enables students to learn at their own pace and in a manner that conforms to their preferences, thereby promoting comprehension and encouraging a sense of ownership in their education. The utilization of ChatGPT thereby fosters a more meaningful and effective connection between students and the learning process, making education more fulfilling and impactful. For example, Bolton College is integrating ChatGPT into its existing Ada chatbot, originally built with IBM Watson, to enhance student support by enabling two-way conversations, improving information retrieval and facilitating personalized course content, which previously resulted in a 3% increase in student retention and an additional £200,000 in revenue.

3.3.2 Lesson planning.

The integration of ChatGPT in lesson planning has the potential to significantly impact the educational landscape, offering numerous benefits to educators. By leveraging ChatGPT’s capabilities, educators can streamline the design of lesson plans and curricula, resulting in a more efficient and creative planning process (Javaid et al., 2023b). This time savings allows educators to shift their focus to refining teaching strategies, engaging with students and addressing individual learning needs. The collaboration between educators and ChatGPT also fosters a dynamic and diversified approach to education, combining the creativity of instructors with the data-driven insights of ChatGPT. This relationship enables the adaptation of lesson plans to cater to the evolving needs of students and the ever-changing educational landscape, ultimately enhancing the overall quality and effectiveness of teaching methods (Nguyen Thi Thu, 2023).

Although ChatGPT possesses impressive abilities in generating teaching materials such as lesson plans and virtual presentations, it cannot serve as a substitute for educators (van den Berg and du Plessis, 2023). Teachers play a critical role in evaluating, enhancing, adjusting and applying these materials effectively in their teaching. According to van den Berg and du Plessis (2023), while ChatGPT can provide precise and relevant information, it lacks the capability to offer emotional support, interaction and the human touch that are essential for effective teaching. Similarly, Shoufan (2023) also noted that ChatGPT may not fully comprehend the text it generates or the context, which could lead to inaccurate responses. Therefore, ChatGPT and other AI models should be regarded as tools that complement and enhance the work of teachers rather than replace them. For example, Basingstoke College of Technology is using Google Gemini and ChatGPT for lesson planning and content creation, aiming to save teachers time while exploring marking and feedback automation with Generative AI tools. The college developed prompt templates to assist educators in effectively using these tools for various teaching purposes and after positive feedback regarding time savings and resource quality. However, staff must remain vigilant about potential inaccuracies and biases in the outputs generated by the AI.

3.3.3 Providing feedback.

ChatGPT’s capacity to provide student feedback carries immense benefits for both educators and learners. According to the findings of Rasul et al. (2023), using its advanced natural language processing abilities, ChatGPT can comprehensively assess and furnish feedback on multiple aspects of student assignments, including grammar, structure and content. This feedback not only identifies errors but also offers valuable suggestions for improvement, enabling students to develop their writing and problem-solving skills (Gürkut et al., 2023). For educators, this represents a considerable time-saving opportunity, as the tedious task of manual grading and feedback provision is expedited, allowing instructors to concentrate on more engaging teaching methods (Gürkut et al., 2023; Hall, 2023). In addition, Javaid et al. (2023b) also noted that incorporation of ChatGPT into the feedback loop leads to a more enriching learning experience, providing students with prompt, detailed and constructive input, ultimately contributing to improved academic performance and overall success in their academic journey.

3.3.4 Creating study materials.

ChatGPT’s utility in generating study materials represents a paradigm shift in the realm of education. Its ability to produce summaries, explanations and study aids for complex subjects is a game-changer, revolutionizing students’ comprehension of course content (Javaid et al., 2023b). The application’s forte lies in its capacity to simplify complex ideas into more manageable parts, thus making challenging topics accessible to learners. This is especially beneficial in subjects with high intricacy, providing students with a clear path to understanding. According to Gimpel et al. (2023), the consistent quality of ChatGPT-generated study materials ensures that students receive uniform, high-quality explanations and summaries, which is particularly valuable in large educational settings with multiple instructors. In addition, Baidoo-Anu and Ansah (2023) also noted that the accessibility of ChatGPT-generated study materials allows students to review and reinforce their grasp of course content at their own pace, promoting efficient learning. Therefore, the incorporation of ChatGPT in the creation of study materials elevates the overall educational experience by providing students with resources that are clear, concise and readily available to support their academic growth. As a result, students’ understanding and performance are likely to improve.

3.3.5 Generating quiz and exam questions.

ChatGPT’s contribution to the assessment domain with its ability to create quiz and exam questions represents a significant advancement, providing instructors with a powerful tool to evaluate students’ knowledge and critical thinking abilities. Hargreaves (2023) stated that a notable advantage of this technology lies in its ability to generate a wide range of diverse and complex assessment items, ensuring a comprehensive evaluation of student understanding. This versatility eliminates the constraints of reusing questions or relying on a limited pool of assessment items and encourages innovative assessment approaches. As a result, assessments become not only a measure of knowledge but also a means to develop and evaluate students’ higher-order thinking skills, such as problem-solving, analysis and synthesis. In their recent work (Rasul et al., 2023), they emphasized that the incorporation of ChatGPT into the formulation of quizzes and exam questions considerably enhances the assessment process. This integration not only amplifies the evaluation process but also provides a more comprehensive range of assessment items that captivate students’ interest and closely align with the intended learning outcomes. This approach also improves their understanding of course material but also equips them with the critical thinking and problem-solving skills necessary for real-world challenges.

3.3.6 Language learning support.

ChatGPT has a profound impact on language learning and offers a comprehensive resource for language instructors to enhance their teaching and assessment of language skills. Its adaptability is a key advantage, as it provides a diverse range of language exercises catering to students’ specific needs, including vocabulary, grammar, pronunciation and conversational abilities (Mohamed, 2023). This customization ensures that students receive tailored language practice according to their unique learning objectives. ChatGPT is also an effective partner for pronunciation improvement and conversational practice, offering accurate feedback and engaging in simulated dialogues, particularly beneficial for language learners seeking to improve their conversational fluency (Atlas, 2023; Javaid et al., 2023b). Moreover, Liu et al. (2023) also stated that ChatGPT extends its language support to the correction of written assignments, providing insights into grammar, syntax and vocabulary, thereby enabling students to refine their written language skills and produce higher-quality written work. According to Kalla and Smith (2023), ChatGPT is a highly efficient aid in language translation services, as it can swiftly generate accurate translations. Its multilingual capabilities allow for translation between a wide range of languages, providing cost-effective and efficient solutions. This AI is equipped to process substantial quantities of text and can easily be integrated into various applications, making it an invaluable asset to the language translation industry (Javaid et al., 2023b). The integration of ChatGPT into language instruction amplifies the overall language learning experience, offering personalized, interactive and efficient language practice, thereby propelling students towards fluency and proficiency in the target language. For example, at King Edward VI School in Stratford-upon-Avon, teachers are experimenting with Generative AI, including ChatGPT and the language learning tool Memrise, to enhance student support by providing personalized language tutoring, facilitating engaging homework tasks and serving as a personal revision assistant for essay planning, while an action research program is being established to further explore the opportunities and challenges posed by AI in education.

3.3.7 Virtual teaching assistance.

ChatGPT’s role as a virtual teaching assistant offers invaluable real-time support to students, particularly beneficial in online or blended learning environments where immediate assistance may be required. Its 24/7 availability ensures that students can access support at any time, creating a continuous and seamless learning experience. The provision of instant answers to student queries streamlines the learning process, empowering students to progress confidently by eliminating potential roadblocks. According to Javaid et al. (2023b), ChatGPT’s virtual teaching assistant role promotes active and independent learning, encouraging students to seek solutions on their own while using ChatGPT as a valuable resource when needed. This self-directed approach fosters essential problem-solving and critical thinking skills, highly applicable in both academic and real-world contexts. In summary, the incorporation of ChatGPT as a virtual teaching assistant enriches the overall learning experience by providing students with continuous support, swift responses to queries and an opportunity to develop independent problem-solving skills, ultimately enhancing their educational journey (Mondal et al., 2023).

3.3.8 Tutoring.

As an AI tutor, ChatGPT plays a vital role in offering students support and guidance in their academic pursuits. Whether students are struggling with specific subjects or topics or seek to reinforce their understanding, ChatGPT serves as a patient and knowledgeable tutor. Its adaptability to individual learning paces allows students to progress at their own speed, seek clarification and revisit topics as needed (Dwivedi et al., 2023). This personalized approach ensures that every student receives the support required to master the subject matter. With 24/7 availability, ChatGPT offers round-the-clock support, catering to students in need of assistance outside regular classroom hours or with urgent queries. Its constant accessibility empowers students to seek help precisely when they need it. Furthermore, Rasul et al. (2023) study on the role of ChatGPT in higher education highlighted that ChatGPT encourages independent learning by motivating students to actively engage with course material, pose questions and find solutions. This self-directed approach cultivates problem-solving skills and a deeper understanding of the subjects they are studying. As an additional resource for comprehension and reinforcement, ChatGPT complements traditional teaching methods, offering students an effective tool for academic success.

3.4 The use of ChatGPT for students assessment in higher education

ChatGPT presents a plethora of possibilities for the utilization in higher education for student assessment, transforming the conventional methods of evaluating and improving students’ academic performance. These applications span a broad spectrum of the assessment process, ranging from automating grading to providing personalized feedback and encouraging critical thinking (Farrokhnia et al., 2023). Each of these applications possesses the potential to simplify and enhance the assessment process for both teachers and students, fostering a more efficient and equitable evaluation process in the digital era. The following subsections provide a more detailed examination of these utilizations. Figure 6 shows the various applications of ChatGPT for students assessment in Higher Education.

3.4.1 Automated grading.

ChatGPT streamlines the grading process in higher education by automating the evaluation of assignments, exams and papers (Atlas, 2023; Javaid et al., 2023b). This automation is particularly advantageous for classes with a large number of students, as it expedites the feedback loop. Instructors can define grading criteria, and ChatGPT will apply these standards consistently, ensuring fairness and accuracy. Cotton et al. (2023) argued that students can benefit from receiving immediate feedback on their work, enabling them to identify areas of improvement and make timely adjustments to their learning strategies. This efficiency not only saves educators valuable time but also empowers students to take a more active role in their academic development by offering prompt insights into their performance.

3.4.2 Essay scoring.

ChatGPT possesses the capability to evaluate essays, presenting a thorough appraisal encompassing aspects such as grammar, coherence and content. Automated essay scoring promotes consistency in grading, eliminating the discrepancies that may arise when multiple instructors assess the same work. ChatGPT’s thorough feedback on essays helps students to identify and work on specific areas that need improvement, thus refining their writing abilities and deepening their comprehension of the topic (Barrot, 2023). This method of essay evaluation aligns with current assessment norms and inspires students to strive for higher levels of writing competence.

3.4.3 Personalized feedback.

ChatGPT’s proficiency in providing detailed and personalized feedback on students’ answers is a noteworthy advantage in higher education. This exceeds mere grading and achieves a deeper understanding of the student’s strengths and weaknesses, enabling tailored suggestions for improvement. ChatGPT can pinpoint common errors, misconceptions or knowledge gaps and recommend resources or additional study materials specific to the individual student’s needs (Kalla and Smith, 2023). This personalized feedback approach fosters student autonomy and self-regulation, permitting them to identify and address their shortcomings, seek additional support and refine their knowledge and skills. Moreover, instructors can benefit from this feature as it highlights common student challenges, thereby aiding in the development of more effective teaching strategies.

3.4.4 Prompting critical thinking.

ChatGPT’s innovative approach to student evaluation, which involves designing assessment questions that prompt critical thinking and problem-solving, represents a progressive step forward in evaluating students (Parker et al., 2024). By formulating questions that compel students to analyze, synthesize and apply information, ChatGPT promotes deeper engagement with the subject matter, moving beyond mere memorization (Dai et al., 2023; Onal and Kulavuz-Onal, 2024). This method of assessment goes beyond rote memorization and tests a student’s ability to think critically and independently, requiring them to explore the subject from various perspectives, make connections and provide well-reasoned responses. As a result, students not only demonstrate their mastery of the subject but also their ability to use that knowledge effectively, equipping them with the skills necessary to tackle the complex problem-solving demands of their future careers.

3.4.5 Enhancing formative assessment.

ChatGPT’s real-time formative feedback has a significant impact on the assessment process in higher education. Formative assessment, which emphasizes tracking student progress and providing timely guidance, is a crucial aspect of effective teaching and learning. According to Dai et al. (2023), ChatGPT offers immediate insights into a student’s performance, highlighting areas of strength and weakness, enabling students to adjust their learning strategies as needed. This, in turn, empowers instructors to adjust their teaching methods to meet the evolving needs of their students, ensuring that the learning experience remains relevant and effective (Parker et al., 2024).

3.4.6 Adaptive assessment.

ChatGPT’s aptitude for creating adaptive assessments represents a significant advancement in higher education (Parker et al., 2024). These evaluations are designed to evolve in response to a student’s performance, ensuring that each individual is evaluated comprehensively and accurately. As the assessment progresses, ChatGPT adapts, presenting more challenging questions if the student is excelling or offering additional guidance and support if the student is struggling. This adaptive approach ensures that the assessment is aligned with each student’s abilities and learning pace, providing a precise measurement of their knowledge and skills while also delivering a supportive and tailored assessment experience. These assessments cater to the diverse needs of students, fostering fair and effective evaluation.

3.5 Limitations and challenges of ChatGPT in education

While deep learning presents numerous opportunities to enhance learning and address a wide array of challenges, it is not without its limitations. These limitations can be difficult to identify due to the technology’s continually evolving nature. To fully capitalize on the benefits of deep learning in education, which include increased efficiency and improved service value, it is crucial to comprehend the technology’s constraints and carefully assess its most promising applications.

An examination of ChatGPT’s utility in medical education revealed a range of considerations, including legal issues, opacity, copyright concerns, misinformation, improper citations and cybersecurity risks (Sallam, 2023). Conversely, the benefits encompass improved scientific writing, research time savings, cost reduction and personalized learning opportunities. It is important to note that, while ChatGPT is proficient in generating scientific content, it occasionally produces unsupported statements (Cooper, 2023), which can lead to plagiarism temptations among students. Therefore, it is essential to promote academic integrity, a strong work ethic and a commitment to authentic learning (Bin-Nashwan et al., 2023).

One notable drawback of ChatGPT is its proneness to generate inaccurate information, which often leads to unpredictable and inordinate output that requires verification (Onal and Kulavuz-Onal, 2024). In the domain of assessments, it is inferior to graduate students in its inability to perform in-depth analysis and distinguish relevant information (Ray, 2023). In addition, its performance is contingent upon the quality and accessibility of the data it learns from, which can come in both structured and unstructured formats. Structured data may contain errors that require human intervention for labeling. Another weakness of ChatGPT is the opacity of its decision-making processes, which often obstructs its ability to explain the rationale behind its predictions and recommendations, particularly in contexts with potential societal and individual impacts (Dwivedi et al., 2023). Furthermore, its capacity to relate abstract concepts in disciplines such as anthropology, psychology, sociology, history and literature is limited, as these subjects involve complex human perspectives and judgment that are less amenable to full automation.

Moreover, a critical constraint that requires attention is the substantial amount of labeled data that deep-learning models demand, which may not always meet the necessary accuracy standards when sourced from online sources. The presence of systemic biases in the data is a further concern, as these may be overlooked despite a company’s values and norms. Intellectual property concerns also persist due to the tendency of the technology to overlook proper attributions. The reliability and availability of information used to generate responses are additional factors that can impact response accuracy. Moreover, ChatGPT’s knowledge of recent events may be limited (Lo, 2023).

Compared to ChatGPT, other AI tools like Copilot, Gemini, Perplexity AI and Jenni serve distinct yet complementary roles in education. For example, Copilot is excellent for coding support, making it invaluable for computer science courses by assisting students with programming and debugging tasks. Perplexity AI focuses on efficient information retrieval, while Gemini enhances learning through multimodal interactions. In contrast, ChatGPT’s adaptability allows it to address a wider range of content generation needs. Recognizing these differences contextualizes ChatGPT’s role in educational technology. While it excels in generating creative assessments and explanations, combining it with specialized tools creates a more comprehensive approach to supporting learning in higher education. 3.6 Ethical implications of ChatGPT and AI in higher education

The integration of ChatGPT into higher education introduces significant ethical considerations that demand careful scrutiny (Kooli, 2023; Mhlanga, 2023; Rasul et al., 2023). Privacy and data protection are major concerns, as ChatGPT processes large amounts of personal data. Institutions must ensure the security of student information and compliance with relevant regulations. Ensuring impartiality is also critical to prevent biases that could disadvantage certain learner groups and reinforce societal inequalities. Transparency and accountability are essential, requiring institutions to clearly communicate how ChatGPT is used and how data is managed, as well as taking responsibility for decisions influenced by AI-generated content. Informed consent is another key aspect, emphasizing the need for students to understand how ChatGPT operates and how their data is handled before consenting to its use. In addition, it is important that ChatGPT’s integration aligns with institutional values, ensuring that it supports educators and promotes collaborative learning.

In practice, reactions to ChatGPT’s presence in education have varied, with some opting for restrictions. For example, Professor Darren Hudson Hick at Furman University reported a case of student plagiarism involving ChatGPT just two weeks after its launch. Furthermore, in January 2023, New York’s public schools banned ChatGPT, followed by similar actions in Los Angeles and Baltimore. Meanwhile, tools like ZeroGPT and Copyleaks have been developed to detect AI-generated content, reflecting concerns about maintaining academic integrity. These measures, however, may prove insufficient as more advanced AI models like GPT-5 emerge. To address these ethical and practical challenges, educators and institutions can adopt several strategies. Developing comprehensive data protection policies and conducting regular audits can ensure compliance with privacy regulations. In addition, providing training to educators and administrators on ethical AI use and data management is crucial. Establishing an oversight committee to monitor ChatGPT’s implementation can help maintain impartiality and fairness. Transparency should be emphasized through clear communication with students about how ChatGPT is used and how their data is managed. Informed consent procedures should be detailed, allowing students to ask questions and provide feedback.

3.7 Discussion

The integration of ChatGPT in higher education offers several potential benefits, including personalized learning experiences, streamlined lesson planning, comprehensive feedback provision and virtual teaching support. Prior studies (e.g., Zhai, 2022; Rahman and Watanobe, 2023; Mondal et al., 2023) highlight the versatility of ChatGPT in adapting to individual student needs, supporting educators and enhancing overall learning experiences. For example, ChatGPT can generate quiz and exam questions, create study materials and assist in language learning, providing educators with a time-efficient means to deliver tailored learning experiences. Its capacity to offer personalized feedback is particularly noteworthy, as it helps students improve their writing and problem-solving skills (Rasul et al., 2023). This capability suggests that ChatGPT can significantly reshape traditional teaching methods, allowing educators to focus more on interactive, student-centered activities and thereby enhance the quality of instruction.

Despite these advantages, it is crucial to acknowledge the limitations and challenges associated with the use of ChatGPT in education. One significant drawback is its tendency to generate inaccurate information, leading to unpredictable outputs that require careful verification (Onal and Kulavuz-Onal, 2024). This limitation underscores the necessity of maintaining human oversight, especially in grading complex assignments, as ChatGPT may not perform in-depth analyses or distinguish relevant information in the same way that experienced educators can (Ray, 2023). In addition, the effectiveness of ChatGPT depends heavily on the quality and accessibility of the data used to train the model. Errors in structured data can necessitate human intervention for correction, reinforcing the need for educators to remain vigilant in evaluating the outputs generated by AI tools.

Moreover, the opacity of ChatGPT’s decision-making processes can hinder its ability to explain the rationale behind its predictions and recommendations (Dwivedi et al., 2023). This lack of transparency is particularly concerning in fields such as anthropology, psychology, sociology, history and literature, where complex human perspectives and judgments are critical. The limitations of ChatGPT in relating abstract concepts become apparent in these disciplines.

The ethical implications of using AI in education also warrant careful consideration. ChatGPT’s handling of personal data raises important questions regarding privacy and data protection, necessitating a commitment to safeguarding student information and complying with relevant regulations (Kooli, 2023; Mhlanga, 2023; Rasul et al., 2023). Institutions must ensure that their use of ChatGPT does not result in discriminatory treatment of specific learner groups or perpetuate existing societal inequalities. Upholding transparency and accountability is essential; educational institutions should provide clear insights into how ChatGPT is used and the decisions influenced by its recommendations.

Informed consent emerges as a crucial ethical dimension, emphasizing the importance of ensuring that students are well-informed about ChatGPT’s usage and data management practices before granting their consent. This commitment to ethical standards must align with the institution’s educational values and objectives, ensuring that the integration of ChatGPT complements the roles of educators and fosters collaborative learning. By addressing these ethical considerations, educational institutions can facilitate the responsible implementation of ChatGPT in ways that resonate with their principles and missions.

To effectively tackle the limitations and ethical challenges posed by ChatGPT, educators and institutions can adopt several practical recommendations. First, developing comprehensive data protection policies and conducting regular audits can help ensure compliance with privacy regulations. Training for educators and administrators on ethical AI usage and data management practices is also crucial. Establishing an oversight committee to monitor the implementation and impact of ChatGPT can help maintain fairness and impartiality in its application.

Transparency is key; institutions should communicate openly with students about how ChatGPT is used and how their data are managed. Informed consent procedures should be clear and detailed, providing opportunities for students to ask questions and provide feedback. Furthermore, ChatGPT should be integrated into the curriculum in ways that align with the institution’s educational values and goals, promoting collaborative learning rather than replacing human educators. Continuous evaluation and improvement of ChatGPT’s integration should be undertaken, using feedback from both students and faculty to make necessary adjustments.

In summary, while the integration of ChatGPT presents numerous opportunities to enhance learning and address challenges within the educational landscape, it is essential to understand the technology’s constraints and implement ethical practices. Striking a balance between leveraging the benefits of AI and addressing its limitations will be key to fostering an educational environment that is both innovative and equitable.

This study has comprehensively examined the evolving role and impact of ChatGPT in higher education through a systematic literature review of 29 scholarly works. The findings underscore ChatGPT’s potential to revolutionize various aspects of the educational process, such as personalized learning, lesson planning, providing feedback, generating study materials, quizzes and exam questions, supporting language learning, virtual teaching assistance, tutoring and fostering critical thinking. These applications highlight the adaptability and versatility of ChatGPT in meeting diverse educational needs, offering opportunities to enhance teaching, learning and assessment practices.

The future of ChatGPT in higher education appears promising, with opportunities for further innovation and refinement. Educators and researchers are urged to continue exploring new ways to leverage AI for improved educational outcomes while fostering open discussions around the ethical implications. With a thoughtful and proactive approach, ChatGPT can play a transformative role in shaping the future of education, making learning more accessible, interactive and tailored to individual needs. As a result, educators can leverage generative AI models like ChatGPT to support student learning. Several pressing questions demand answers: How can we harness ChatGPT to enrich student learning? How can we integrate generative AI tools into teacher education programs to prepare prospective teachers to effectively use AI tools in their classrooms? Will these generative AI tools bridge or exacerbate the existing digital divide, and what is the path forward? Therefore, it is not only impracticable but also detrimental to deny the significance of AI’s impact on higher education. Although there are challenges associated with AI, it is crucial to recognize that higher education can exist and even flourish alongside advancements in AI technology, as long as we adapt our pedagogy and evaluation strategies to this technological advancement. Policymakers, researchers, educators and technology experts should collaborate and initiate discussions on how these evolving generative AI tools can be used safely and constructively to enhance education and bolster student learning.
